Genre1-poetry

Hypertext poetry can consist of words, although not necessarily organized into lines and stanzas, as well as, sounds, visual images, movement or other special effects. Although the poem may dazzling with sounds, perhaps of a lawnmower, while the words "mowing," "stop," "Sunday," and "morning" float across your computer screen in pseudo-three dimensional letters, one will have be hard pressed to identify the use of any formal poetics. There are an infinite number of new and unusual effects that can be superimposed on text to create a hypertext poem, but below are some examples of types of "clickable" poems.

Older and Far Away by Danah Henriksen

I very much enjoyed the process of working on this assignment, as it provided an opportunity to try something different and new. My hypertext writing is in the form of poetry, so in prefacing my project I should say that I have never before written poetry (at least that I can remember), and have never written anything specifically for hypertext. The feeling of inexperience with both the medium and the mode of expression was a bit daunting at first, but I did not want to miss the opportunity to try something novel,and so decided upon hypertext poetry. My project takes on a simple structure with fairly short rhymed poems, in which particular words or concepts within the poems are links to other titles.
